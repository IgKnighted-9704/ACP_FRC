Founded in 1989 by Dean Kamen, FIRST Robotics Competition is an international robotics competition for high schoolers that seeks to foster interest and provide real-world experience in science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM).

Given 6 weeks, teams are tasked to create a robot that fulfills the objectives of each year's competition. Through a rigorous and competitive experience, FIRST students are able to work with mentors and professionals in the STEM field. The FIRST competition builds teamwork, business, leadership, and STEM skills.
